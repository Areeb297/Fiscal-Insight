## Why it feels wrong

You entered:

* Salary = 5000
* Months = 1

That usually means:

* this employee costs **SAR 5,000 for 1 month**
* two employees together cost **SAR 10,000 for that month**

So for **1 client for 1 month**, the expected numbers would more likely be:

* **Monthly total team cost** = SAR 10,000
* **Per client monthly cost** = SAR 10,000
* At 30% margin:

  * Selling price ex. VAT = 10,000 ÷ 0.70 = **SAR 14,285.71 / month**
* VAT 15% = **SAR 2,142.86 / month**
* Selling price incl. VAT = **SAR 16,428.57 / month**

## The likely bug

The app is probably doing this:

* `employee total = salary × months`
* summing all employee totals
* treating that sum as a **yearly figure**
* then dividing by 12 again

That is only correct if:

* the input represents **annual total cost**, or
* the employee rows are meant to build a 12-month plan already

It is **not** correct if `Months = 1` means “this cost applies for one actual month of service.”

## What the logic should be

You need to decide what **Months** means.

### Option 1: Months means contract duration in months

Then:

* Employee total = `salary × months`
* Projection total cost = sum of employee totals
* If the whole plan duration is also 1 month, then:

  * monthly cost = total cost
* If plan duration is 12 months, then:

  * monthly average = total cost ÷ 12 only when appropriate

### Option 2: Salary is already monthly salary

Then for “1 month” rows:

* monthly team cost should be computed directly from monthly salaries
* yearly should be `monthly × 12` only if those employees are intended for all 12 months
