You should **divide shared cost by 4**, not multiply it by 4.

From your current projection:

* Team monthly cost is **SAR 5,001.00** for Atif over the engagement. 
* Total overhead is **SAR 469.17 / month**. 
* With **4 clients**, the app is currently showing **team cost / client = SAR 1,250.25** and **overhead / client = SAR 117.29**, which is exactly the shared-cost approach. 
* That leads to **total cost / client = SAR 1,367.54** and then **recommended selling price = SAR 1,953.63 / client / month** at **30% margin**.

So the correct logic for **shared department costs** is:

* First calculate total monthly shared cost
* Then divide by number of clients
* Then apply margin to get per-client selling price
* Then multiply selling price by number of clients to get total revenue

In your case, the shared-cost math is:

* Shared monthly cost = **5,001.00 + 469.17 = 5,470.17**.
* Per-client cost = **5,470.17 ÷ 4 = 1,367.54**
* Per-client sell price at 30% margin = **1,367.54 ÷ 0.70 = 1,953.63**
* Total monthly revenue = **1,953.63 × 4 = 7,814.52**, which matches your card.

So for the main department/team section, the current behavior is conceptually right.

Where you **would multiply by 4** is only when the resource is **per client**, not shared.

Use this rule:

* **Shared resource**: divide across all clients
* **Dedicated per-client resource**: multiply by client count
* **Partially shared / partially dedicated**: split by allocation basis

A simple example:

* One account manager serving all 4 clients → shared → divide by 4
* One support engineer needed for each client → per client → multiply by 4
* PM at 10% across all clients → shared → divide by 4 after applying allocation
* Dedicated .NET resource for one client only → assign only to that client, do not spread equally

That is the main design improvement your app still needs: every cost row should have an **allocation basis**.

## What to add to the model

For each employee or managed-service resource, add:

* `Allocation %`
* `Cost Basis`

  * Shared across all clients
  * Per client
  * Assigned clients count
  * Specific client
* `Billable %` if needed
* `Start month`
* `End month` or duration

Then formulas become:

### Shared department resource

`allocated_monthly_cost = monthly_ctc × alloc_percent`

`per_client_cost = allocated_monthly_cost ÷ active_clients`

### Per-client resource

`allocated_monthly_cost = monthly_ctc × alloc_percent × assigned_clients`

### Selling price

`selling_price_per_client = per_client_cost ÷ (1 - margin_percent)`

## My recommendation on your exact question

For **Atif’s SAR 5,001/month**, if he is one shared department resource serving all 4 clients, then:

* **do not multiply his cost by 4**
* keep total monthly team cost at **SAR 5,001**
* divide by **4 clients**

That is the whole point of this app: make sure shared costs are covered collectively by the portfolio, while still showing profit per client and in total. Your current per-client math appears aligned with that approach.
